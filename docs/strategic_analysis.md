## II. THE "CMMI LEVEL 5" DILEMMA

### The Analysis
*   **Standard Definition**: CMMI Level 5 ("Optimizing") implies an *organization* that continuously changes its processes based on statistical data.
*   **BA-Kit Reality**: BA-Kit provides the *tools* to do this (SPC Charts, Root Cause Analysis), but it cannot force the user to change.
*   **The Risk**: Claims of "Being CMMI Level 5" may sound like "Marketing Fluff" to seasoned Enterprise Architects.
*   **The Pivot**:
    *   **Old Claim**: "We are a CMMI Level 5 System."
    *   **New Truth**: "We enable **High-Assurance Business Analysis** (inspired by CMMI Level 5)."
    *   **Nuance**: Start focusing on **"Standards-Based agents"** (Agents that know ISO, IIBA, PMI) rather than just "Process Maturity".

## III. THE "SWARM" (BẦY ĐÀN) LINGUISTIC FALLACY

### The Problem
*   **English "Swarm"**: implies collective intelligence, synchronized movement, cool sci-fi vibe.
*   **Vietnamese "Bầy đàn"**: implies instinct, insects, chaos, lack of leadership ("tâm lý bầy đàn" = herd mentality, often negative).
*   **Conflict**: We are selling **System 2** (Deliberate, Critical Thinking). "Bầy đàn" implies **System 1** (Reactive, Instinctive).

### The Solution: "The Squad" (Biệt Đội)
We must shift the metaphor from **Biology (Insects)** to **Special Operations (Elite Team)**.

| Concept | Old Term (Vi) | New Term (Vi) | Why? |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **System Name** | Bầy Đàn (Swarm) | **Biệt Đội (Squad / Task Force)** | Professional, Elite, Organized. |
